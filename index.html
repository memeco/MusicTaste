Hello, I'm Emerson Marques Pedro, 26 years old, social scientist from SÃ£o Paulo, Brazil, and I'd like share a story with you:

In late 2012, I had everything set up for applying for a masters degree at the University of Amsterdam this year. It turns out that I was diagnosed with an autoimmune neurological disease with no cure and of unknown causes: multiple sclerosis. Fortunately, where I am from (Brazil), the public healthcare system offers free treatment for it. It usually costs up to $3,000 per month.

Given that I had to cancel my original plan of a masters abroad because of it, I came up with an alternate plan: my masters thesis was about Internet Culture, so why not start a website and try an open collaborative research project instead?

It's original title is/was "The music taste of the digital natives generation", and it mainly revolves around the concept of "social distinction" by Pierre Bourdieu + the criticism of that theory using the concept of "omnivorousness". My hypothesis basically relies on the idea that "the digital natives are 'cultural omnivorous' due to the fact that they are displayed a wider range of cultural goods on the web", or in other words, geeks are generally more eclectic (music-wise, at least) than the average listener.

I plan to use data from the Last.fm API at first (perhaps Spotify, Pandora, and Grooveshark APIs too), then some interviews and questionaries to check and prove my hypothesis. This is going to be at first, of course, an exploratory research, later we'll discuss what we can come up next with the results and analysis of it.

I couldn't find any specific research related to this topic (sociology of taste + internet culture), but please feel free to send me any related studies. =]
